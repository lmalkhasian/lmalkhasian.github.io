DJ Recess

My Role

Kinect Gesture Developer

The Team

Michael Cope
Evan Netzer
Devin Smith
Brad Styes
Levi Lambright (art)

Dates

January to April 2012

DJ Recess is a game on the Microsoft Kinect platform, which is aimed toward children with Autism Spectrum Disorder. I developed this game with 4 other students for one of my senior design classes at the University of Michigan. Our goals were to make a fun game that would provide some therapeutic value to our audience.

Role Description

My primary role on the team was to develop the high-level gesture recognition for the game play.

Process

We began by researching Autism via online materials and interviews with family members of children with Autism. When we finalized that we wanted our game to involve therapy via music teaching, we began developing the game. Once we had valid and working game, we tested it with several autistic children and their families. Given their feedback and suggestions, we made improvements to the design.
