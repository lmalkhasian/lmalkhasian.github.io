About the Somali Bantu Association's Website Redesign

I partnered with the passionate people of the Somali Bantu Association of America to redesign their website into a more engaging experience for users. The Somali Bantu Association is a non-profit organization that works with Somali Bantu and other African refugees to help them with their transition into western culture. After doing a bit of research into what they do, I redesigned their website and provided them with mockups and UI specs.

Process

Goals for the Redesign

The first thing I did was learn about the organization and outline goals for the redesign with the Somali Bantu Association.

  1. Design a simple, clear and concise navigation system.
  2. Make the website more engaging by using more images and stories of the Somali Bantu, and the people who work at the SBAOA.
  3. Explain the Somali Bantu Association's role in the community--what services do they provide?
  4. Build empathy by explaining why Somali Bantu refugees need aid when coming to America.
  5. Design the information on the current website to be more skimmable and digestible.
  6. Revamp the website's aesthetic.
  7. Make donating and signing up for the newsletter highly accessible to encourage users to stay engaged with the organization after they leave the website.

Literature Review and Guerilla Interviews

I did a bit of research to learn about the non-profit space, and what people want to do on non profit websites. I did this by 1. Reading up online and 2. Quick guerilla interviews with people who I knew.

Personas and Use Cases

I created 2 personas from the 2 key use cases I found during my research to ground the designs in users’ real needs.


See the full document of personas and use cases here.

Information Design

The first thing I wanted to design was the website’s content. I went through every word of the website and edited it to be more concise, and better organized. I used a bottom up approach to create a navigation plan for existing and new content.

Low-fidelity Concept Sketches

Then I moved onto creating sketches and concepts for the website.


Mockups, Testing, and UI Specs

I digitized the sketches to create mid-fidelity mockups. Then, I tested those mockups with 2 potential users, made a few changes, and then created UI Specs to help guide the development of the new site.
