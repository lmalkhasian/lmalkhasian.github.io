About

My capstone project focuses on Eaton Switchgear, which are the large gray boxes that manage power supplies going into buildings. My team's goal is to create the future of switchgear-human interaction.

Me interviewing a hospital electrician.

Process


Research

We interviewed 33 domain experts.

Me looking at a vintage motor control center.

And toured 4 university and hospital basements to see the switchgears and ask questions as we toured.

A small portion of our affinity diagram.
Synthesis

We synthesized over 600 notes from our field research into one affinity diagram, two customer journey maps, and four cultural diagrams.

Putting all of our findings together.

As a final step in our synthesis, we connected our high level findings to create a story.

Our switchgear story. It highlights that in the coming years, switchgear operators are going to interact with the gear less and less.
Visioning

We spent a lot of time visioning and coming up with cool, futuristic ideas that included wearables, google glass, augmented reality, etc. In the end, we decided to pursue a less flashy, and more valuable solution.

Our goal is to bring value to all of the data that facilities collect with smart notifications, and recommendations.

We're making the assumption that in the future, sensors are going to be more pervasive and reliable, and the "internet of things" very real. We're currently pursuing how all of these sensors can be used to give power operations people valuable notifications.

Iteration 3 prototypes that I created.
Prototyping

We started prototyping with lo-fidelity paper prototypes and have moved to clickable prototypes.

Myself and Christi testing a clickable prototype with a potential end user in Houston.
Usability Testing

We are meeting with at least 5 potential users during every iteration to get feedback from real people!

Team Lumin.

Next Steps
