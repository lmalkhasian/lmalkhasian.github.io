Final Concept

Waiter, Please is a concept for a table that lights up towards the direction of your waiter only when you need her and she is nearby! This allows you to enjoy your beer without having to constantly look around for your waiter. It also maintains the human to human communication and value that waiters provide such as recommendations on house specialties.

UP! On the Wednesday that we did our observations.

Process

Research

We started by doing observations and guerrilla interviews at UP - a local bar/restaurant in Pittsburgh on wine night!

Here are some of our key findings:

  • People come and stay with their friends.
  • It's difficult to get the bartender's attention.
  • People always go to the bar, right after they walk in.
  • Having an intimate conversation is really difficult because of the music level.
A diagram of how we rated our ideas.
Visioning

We came up four framings for the opportunity space:

  • People are stuck in their own groups.
  • People have trouble ordering food and drinks.
  • Multiple atmospheres are needed to meet different people’s needs.
  • Groups come to celebrate special events.

We created 12 solutions for these framings, and then narrowed those down to our best 6 based on feasibility, originality, impact and feedback from peers.

One of the storyboards we speed dated. Credit: Angela
Speed Dating

When we introduced our storyboards to people, and got their feedback on our ideas, we found a couple new things.

  • People don’t really want to meet new people at the bar. Especially women.
  • People find getting a waiter or waitresses attention the most challenging when they are trying to get the check to leave.
The rough cut of our video we created in our office space!
Video Sketch

Based on these new findings, we created Waiter, Please. A system that helps people locate their wait staff but enables them to make the decision of whether they want to flag them down or not.

We created a rough cut of our concept video after we sketched out all of the screens we would need. After getting feedback about production and content, we moved on to our hi-fidelity video.
